## [Metallicity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metallicity)

 The details of this progression depend sensitively on 
* the mass of the star
* the metallicity of the star

The **metallicity** of a star is the amount of matter in the star that has an atomic number higher than helium: Let $X$ denote the total fraction of the mass of a star that is hydrogen (or a hydrogen ion or isotope). Let $Y$ denote that fraction that is helium (or a helium ion/isotope). Let $Z$ denote the fraction of the total mass that is anything else. Then by definition 
$$
X+Y+Z=1
$$
since any part of the mass is either hydrogenic, heliogenic, or something else. The metallicity refers to the fraction $Z$. 

As we will discuss in `u_cosmology`, immediately after the big bang, the Universe was composed of about 75% hydrogen/protons and 25% helium (and the slightest amount of lithium). Therefore, the oldest stars of the universe-called **Population III** stars should essentially have $Z_\textrm{III}=0$, $X_\textrm{III}= 0.75$, and $Y_\textrm{III} = 0.25$. Solar values, on the other hand, are $(X_\odot, Y_\odot, Z_\odot)=(0.744, 0.242,0.014)$.
